Overview\/Introduction<\/p>\n\n\n\n

With the help of Synology, I am embarking on the creation of a portable lab environment called CloudyRange. The objective of CloudyRange is to create a high performance lab environment that is small enough to fit in my backpack when traveling for work and be a small “cloud” as you may never know where the data is at any point… At its core, the environment will consist of the following hardware:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \n
  • 1 x Synology DS723 (2 x 960GB SSD)<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • 2 x NUC 11 (Core i7, 48GB RAM and 2TB SSD)<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • 1 x Unifi Flex Mini (Switch)<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • 1 x GL.iNet (Router, VPN and Access Point)<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

    Over the next three or four blog posts, we will look at the configuration items and requirements to build an ultra portable home lab that still has enough horsepower and flexibility to tackle any lab needs. This environment will be called “CloudyRange” and is inspired by faculty and staff at my alma mater.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
